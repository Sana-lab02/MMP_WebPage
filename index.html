The Process

Advanced Thixomolding Advantages

Thixomolding is a semi-solid (Thixotropic) molding process for magnesium somewhat analogous to plastic injection molding.

Magnesium chips are fed at ambient temperature into a heated barrel and propelled forward by a rotating screw.

The combined heat from resistance type heater bands and the shearing force provided by the rotating screw create a slurry of spheroidal solid particles within a molten matrix.

This slurry is forced into a pre-heated mold to produce high precision, ultra-thin wall net or near net shaped parts.

The process combines the benefits of die casting and plastic injection molding.

01

Processes a few pounds of material at a time rather than melting hundreds of pounds in large crucibles. Uses a fraction of the energy required for standard die casting.

02

Magnesium is completely contained within the processing machine barrel during production, drastically reducing exposure risks to facility operators and surrounding systems.

03

Semi-solid processing uses lower temperatures and superior flow. Multiple client mold tools exceed 500,000 production shots today with virtually zero maintenance.

04

Superior dimensional stability and shot-to-shot repeatability over conventional die casting. Easily meets strict NADCA Precision Tolerances via computerized process monitoring.

05

Minimizes ecological footprint via maximized material yield, near-zero evaporation, and drastically reduced oxidation compared to legacy metal molding platforms.

06

Delivers a highly uniform globular microstructure that eliminates the structural shrinkage voids, porosity, and dendritic weaknesses common in traditional die casting.

Industries Served

Supporting demanding industries with precision magnesium components.

Automotive

Weight Reduction

Used to reduce vehicle weight and improve fuel economy. Typical applications include structural brackets, mounts, and high-specification cosmetic trim pieces.

Electronics

Precision Equipment

Magnesium, with its strength and natural EMI Sheilding properties, is a great material for electronics enclosures as well as frames for electronic components in laptops & tablets.

Aerospace

Strength-to-Weight

The strength-to-weight-ratio of magnesium alloys makes it a great choice for use in aerospace applications.

Medical

Bioabsorbable Alloys

Magnesium can be used in medical devices as well as in temporary implants. Alloys are available with bioabsorbable properties. See NanoMag for more information.

Led Lighting

Thermal Management

Heat Sinks can be designed using thixomolded magnesium that reduce weight in lighting assemblies as well as disipate heat.
